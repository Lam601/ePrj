Introduction

Here at Bamboo, we simply LOVE elephants and if you do too, this tour is for you! Starting in the stunning northern city of Chiang Mai you'll spend time at two of our supported elephant projects, each with very different itineraries and reasons for doing what they do. You'll then travel to the coffee capital of Chiang Rai and on to the hustle and bustle of Bangkok. Once your time in Thailand is coming to a close, as a special treat, we'll hop you across the Thailand-Cambodian border to the magical city of Siem Reap for an unforgettable sunrise trip to one of the Seven Wonders of the World – Angkor Wat...you lucky, lucky people!

We've dedicated our lives to aid NGOs and private, elephant-based projects across Thailand and will continue to do so as long as it needs to be done. Whilst there is no single set approach that all projects must follow, we have undertaken hours upon hours of due-diligence to ensure that the projects we support offer the right mixture of healthcare, support, exercise and love for these majestic beasts. This tour has been designed to show you two of the most amazing elephant project-types in Thailand. At both locations, you will be educated as to why this project exists, its primary goals and objectives and how your money supports the work that happens. At the end of your time with each of the projects, we'll hold a group Q&A to discuss your thoughts, ideas and suggestions. The reason that this tour has been created is that there is so much media coverage of elephants in Thailand, we feel to give you a completely non-biased overview of two of the most common project types will allow you to make up your own mind on this very emotive subject. You will leave the tour very well informed and can then make up your own mind, rather than be led by what you might read or see across social media.

And finally, we hope that during this 14-day tour, we hope you will have a fun and exciting experience.

Itinerary

Come one, come all to the remote city of Chiang Mai. Whether you’ve flown here or managed to sail halfway across the world and hike through miles of rainforest, our staff will greet you upon your entry and whisk you away to your hotel near the old city gates. From there, have a shower, relax, or venture off into the heart of this temple town. The day is yours to explore. If you’re up for it tonight, we’ll have an informal meet-and-greet with dinner, some drinks, some laughter, another drink or two before passing out from the jet-lag.

Meals: You’re on your own today.

Since the reign of the Lanna Kingdom over 500- years-ago, a cunning metropolis has developed around ancient Chiang Mai. The time begins with a brief orientation then tour of the city, where we’ll leisurely wander through the backstreets, markets and temples that deem this town “The Rose of the North.” Pit stops at the Wai Sri Suphan and Doi Suthep are remarkable enough to consider joining the monastery by mid-day—please, tell us beforehand, though. Our day concludes with a proper dinner at the famous Night Market, where we’ll class it up for at least one night, and then wander into the night, perhaps to the hilarious, world-famous drag show, when this sleepy country town wakes up.

Meals: Breakfast, lunch and dinner

Breakfast early before jaunting outside of Chiang Mai and into the Thai rainforest to the Elephant location 1, an eco-tourism project launched by local hill-tribes to protect the welfare of elephants. For the next few days, we’ll befriend, feed, bathe, and cuddle the elephants, all while planting food and building a proper ecosystem for the mammoth mammals. Today, our first day, the “Mahouts” (the elephants’ human guardians) will supply their secret elephant handshake you’ll use to greet the animals. What you’ll soon realize, though, is the handshake matters less than what’s in your other hand. Like humans, the way to an elephant’s heart is through its stomach, so you’ll be feeding the creatures plenty of fruits and vegetables, while simultaneously hugging, cuddling, selfie-ing, and daydreaming about your future life together with baby elephants—marriage, more baby elephants, taking little Dumbo to school. Once fed their daily diet of 300 kg (660 lbs.) of fruits, bamboo, and hopefully nobody’s arm (they don’t eat arms), you’ll parade the animals for their ceremonial elebath. Your day ends muddied and in a traditional jungle house. It’s rough-necking, but that’s life out here with the elephants. Totally worth it. Please note for cultural reasons there are a few clothing requirements whilst at the project. You will get the chance to swim with and bath the elephants (be aware this will cause serious feelings of jealousy from your friends and family at home). Whilst in the river you will need to pair your swimming costume with shorts; if a bikini is more your style you will need a t-shirt on too. When walking around the camp please make sure you have either your shoulders or knees covered at all times.

Meals: Breakfast lunch and dinner

Yesterday may have been about feeding and hugging the elephant population, but today concern is sustaining the species. With the help from the Mahouts, we’ll be planting sugar cane, bamboo, and bananas to keep the elephant pantry stocked. We can’t have them eating arms, right? You may also be involved in some community development work such as building local vet clinics. By midday, we’ll probably have sweat off a few kilos, so we’ll refuel with a traditional Thai lunch and a dip under a waterfall in the rainforest’s river. Only clichés can sufficiently describe this beauty, so we’ll just say, “it’s majestic.” At the day’s end, fully exhausted, we’ll come together for dinner and drinks, discussing our time with the elephants and realizing that the only purity left in this world is in the eyes of a baby elephant splashing in the mud.

Meals: Breakfast lunch and dinner

Weather permitting, our final day in this elephant location begins a little bit differently: Bamboo rafting along the river. The jungle scenery and stoic cliffs illuminate just how removed from the Western world we are. Once returned, we’ll wave goodbye to our newest best friend, the elephants, obviously, and return to Chiang Mai, where a celebratory drink, perhaps a tear, and a night of memories awaits.

Meals included: Breakfast and lunch

This morning we will take a 3 - 4 hour drive to our second elephant location. The elephants here have been taken from a life of inadequate health care, poor diet, exhaustion and entertaining tourists; they were unable to just be elephants. These wonderful creatures have been brought back to their natural environment where they are living in semi-wild conditions, free to roam as much as possible and live their lives how they choose – interacting and foraging. Whilst here we will trek through the jungle to find the elephants and observe their natural behaviour's in their home environment. You will have a chance to teach English to the people of the village as well as receive education about elephants in Asia and learn about the local tribal language and cultures. You may also have the chance to learn how to basket weave! On the afternoon of day 8, you will return back to Chiang Mai city for one final night before our next location!

Meals: day 6 - Breakfast lunch and dinner day 7 - Breakfast lunch and dinner day 8 - Breakfast and lunch

Today we will be heading to Chiang Rai the sister city of Chiang Mai, 33 years older but still just as beautiful!

Chiang Rai is actually the oldest city of Thailand and today we will take a look at some of the places that make this area so special!

We will begin with a visit to the famous White Temple where the uniqueness of it will blow you away. After being blown away, we will make our way to the impressive Black temple and then end

our temple tour with the vivid Blue temple. In the afternoon, we will say goodbye to the beautiful city and head back to Chiang Mai where you have the evening to explore the night markets and shops!

Meals included: Breakfast

After grabbing a quick breakfast, we will jet you off to the bustling big city of Bangkok, which is home to over 8 million people. The diversity of the city is one to take in and explore.

Once landed, we will make our way to the lovely guesthouse that is nestled in the famous Khao San Road area. Gear up for all things Hangover related. The day is yours to explore, eat, drink and even party!

Meals: Breakfast

Bangkok’s sterling skyscrapers exude the wealth of Hong Kong while the hidden markets and alleyways take us to old-world Asia, where you can haggle over delectable spices or even a Rolex. After breakfast, we’ll begin the day with an short city orientation to ease any anxieties over the next part of our tour—we get it, it’s like the Wild West out here, except it smells like delicious, delicious fried noodles. From there, we’ll venture out into the sun-filled streets for a half-day tour of the city’s temples along with a trip onto a floating market. It’s a sensory overload for the eyes, ears, mouth, everything, really. Come evening, we’ll join for another welcome meal. So, put on your finest and be ready to experience the flavors of this beguiling city.

Meals: Breakfast and Dinner

Today we will take another short flight to a whole new country, Cambodia! The gateway to the temples of Angkor, Siem Reap embodies the wealth and greatness of old Cambodia, back when gold flowed into the largest ancient city the world has known. Upon arrival, we’ll drop off our bags at the hotel and head into the city to our own non-profit cafe for an Cambodian orientation, along with a group dinner to taste traditional Khmer food whilst doing a bit of people -watching on Pub Street.

Meals: Breakfast

After thoroughly exhausting ourselves on our night out on Pub Street, we’ll be up and out early this morning to Angkor Wat for a sunrise tour (the best time to see the temples, by far). We’ll wander to Bayon Temple and Ta Prohm and take endless selfies in the rising light. It’ll be romantic and breathtaking and a list of twohundred other adjectives that all mean it’s-sobeautiful-I-want-to-livehere-forever.

Meals: Breakfast

Two weeks – gone in the blink of an eye. Don’t mind our sniffling. The tour may have ended but the stories you’ll soon tell are only beginning. It can take weeks to realize what all you accomplished in such a short time with Bamboo. Rather than saying goodbye, we prefer to say, “see you soon.” You’re part of The BamFam now.

Meals: Breakfast
